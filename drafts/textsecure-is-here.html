TextSecure is here

Published: Thu 27 February 2014
By lxgr

In misc.

tags: security cryptography messaging

Would you like to use a mobile instant messaging service that works just like the one with the greeen and white bubble icon, but provides end-to-end encryption? I've been looking for something like that for quite a while, and so I'm very happy that two days ago, TextSecure finally released their major Android client update.

Instead of SMS, it now uses Google's (and in the future Apple's) push messaging services in order to not drain your device's battery, but the payload is encrypted with the recepient's private key, so you don't have to trust any of the intermediaries.

Of course, there is a lot of things that can go wrong when implementing cryptographic schemes, but WhatsApp is not only designed by people who know what they are doing – both the client and the server are open source software, so you don't have to take their word for it.

Right now, there are still a few rough edges (the balance between ease of use and configurability and of course security is always hard to strike), but it looks really promising – I was already able to convince a few friends to give it a try.
